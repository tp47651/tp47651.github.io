About Me

In my life, I have traveled the world with family and friends; I have lived in more than one country over my lifetime. I wasn’t born in America; I was born in Israel, which is where my story begins. It starts in the first grade when my parents decided to start the change, move to a completely different country, “The New World”. However, this was just the start.

I spent my elementary school years living in America. After a couple of year my family decided to move back to Israel. This move was not a big deal for me back then for the reason being that I was too young to really care about it, I was happy and willing to go to new places and explore. My middle school and high school years flew by right in front of my eyes and before I know it i'm not a little kid anymore. I’m sixteen, I have responsibilities, and I have built a life here back in my country. I could never imagine leaving all of what built me to be who I am today and never look back. Out of nowhere, I had no choice, my parents let me know that we are moving to America yet again, but now to Atlanta. I knew this move was not going to be the same. I am no longer a young kid in elementary school that doesn't know why he is where he is. I have this whole life that I have gotten used to over these years, I thought I have found who I truly am, and why I was put on this earth. Out of the blue, I get slapped by reality that nothing goes as planned in life, at least that's what I think after all that's happened. At sixteen moving to a country and a state that I know nothing about wasn't as simple as I thought, or at least how I hoped it would be. On the other hand, I could take advantage of my situation, I move to a new country, start fresh. I could do whatever I pleased to do this was a new page in my life, a fresh start. I did end up finding myself. I took this opportunity, and I never let go. This move was by far the hardest most irritating thing I have gone through in my life. However, I never gave up.
